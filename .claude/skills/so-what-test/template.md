## Summary for Non-Experts

### 1. What has been proven

[2-3 sentences stating the strongest, most reliable finding as a direct claim with no hedging. Write as established fact. Make it concrete and specific enough that a non-expert understands what was discovered.]

**Example**:
*"This research proves that people make significantly worse decisions when they're sleep-deprived, even after just one night of poor sleep. The effect is large enough to matter in real-world settings like driving, medical diagnosis, and financial choices. This isn't just correlation - controlled experiments show that lack of sleep directly impairs judgment."*

---

### 2. What is still unknown

[2-3 sentences stating honestly and specifically what the field has not yet figured out. Be clear about why this gap exists. Don't minimize the uncertainty or pretend it's smaller than it is.]

**Example**:
*"Researchers still don't know whether the effects of sleep deprivation are permanent or fully reversible with recovery sleep. The gap exists because tracking people's cognitive function over years or decades is extremely difficult and expensive. Without this knowledge, we can't say whether chronic sleep loss causes lasting damage or just temporary impairment."*

---

### 3. Why it matters

[2-3 sentences stating the single most important real-world implication. If there's a practical application, state it clearly. If there's no direct application, state the biggest theoretical consequence. Make it concrete - how does this change what people should do, or how we understand the world?]

**Example**:
*"This matters because millions of people routinely operate vehicles, medical equipment, and other safety-critical systems while sleep-deprived. If we understand how sleep loss impairs decision-making, we can design better work schedules, warning systems, and policies to prevent accidents. It also explains why tired people make poor financial decisions, which has implications for everything from personal finance advice to when important meetings should be scheduled."*

---

## Plain Language Test

**Can a non-expert understand this?**
- ☐ Yes - No jargon, clear examples, concrete claims
- ☐ Mostly - A few terms might need clarification
- ☐ No - Too technical, needs simplification

**Does it avoid hedging?**
- ☐ Yes - States what's known and unknown directly
- ☐ Mostly - Some unnecessary qualifiers
- ☐ No - Too many "may," "might," "suggests"

**Is it specific?**
- ☐ Yes - Concrete examples and clear claims
- ☐ Mostly - Some vague areas
- ☐ No - Too abstract, needs concrete details
