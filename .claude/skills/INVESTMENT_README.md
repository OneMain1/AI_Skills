# Investment Analysis Skills

A comprehensive suite of 10 Claude Code skills for institutional-grade stock analysis and portfolio management, modeled after frameworks from top investment firms.

## Overview

These skills transform how you analyze investments by providing structured protocols based on methodologies from Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, Bridgewater, JPMorgan, BlackRock, Citadel, Harvard Endowment, Bain, Renaissance Technologies, and McKinsey.

### What Are These Skills?

Each skill is a specialized analysis protocol that Claude Code can execute for your investment research. They're designed to work individually or as a complete workflow for comprehensive investment analysis - from screening stocks to building portfolios to timing trades.

### Quick Start

1. Identify your investment goal (screening, valuation, risk management, etc.)
2. Invoke relevant skills using `/skill-name` commands
3. Receive professional-grade analysis following institutional frameworks
4. **IMPORTANT**: This is educational - always verify data and consult financial advisors

## The 10 Investment Skills

### 1. Goldman Sachs Stock Screener (`/goldman-stock-screener`)

**What it does**: Screens stocks based on your criteria and produces top 10 picks
- Creates stock table with comprehensive metrics
- Analyzes P/E ratios vs. sector averages
- Evaluates revenue growth trends (5-year)
- Assesses debt-to-equity and financial health
- Rates competitive moats (weak/moderate/strong)
- Provides bull/bear 12-month price targets
- Assigns risk ratings (1-10 scale)
- Suggests entry zones and stop-loss levels

**When to use**:
- Building investment watchlist
- Screening for specific criteria (value, growth, dividends)
- Finding candidates for deeper analysis
- Comparing stocks across sectors

**Output**: Professional equity research screening report with summary table

---

### 2. Morgan Stanley DCF Valuation (`/morgan-dcf-valuation`)

**What it does**: Builds detailed discounted cash flow models
- Projects 5-year revenue with growth assumptions
- Estimates operating margins based on trends
- Calculates year-by-year free cash flow
- Determines WACC (cost of capital)
- Computes terminal value (two methods)
- Creates sensitivity table (different discount rates)
- Compares DCF value to current market price
- Provides verdict: undervalued/fairly valued/overvalued
- Identifies assumptions that could break the model

**When to use**:
- Determining intrinsic value of specific stock
- Assessing margin of safety at current price
- Understanding key value drivers
- Building detailed investment thesis

**Output**: Investment banking-style valuation memo with full DCF model

---

### 3. Bridgewater Risk Analysis (`/bridgewater-risk-analysis`)

**What it does**: Comprehensive portfolio risk assessment
- Analyzes correlation between holdings
- Assesses sector concentration with percentages
- Evaluates geographic/currency risk
- Tests interest rate sensitivity
- Conducts recession stress test (estimated drawdown)
- Rates liquidity risk for each holding
- Recommends position sizing
- Estimates tail risk scenarios with probabilities
- Suggests top 3 hedging strategies
- Provides specific rebalancing recommendations

**When to use**:
- Reviewing current portfolio risk
- Before adding new positions
- During market volatility
- Preparing for potential downturns
- Understanding hidden correlations

**Output**: Professional risk management report with heat map summary

---

### 4. JPMorgan Earnings Analysis (`/jpmorgan-earnings-analysis`)

**What it does**: Pre-earnings preview or post-earnings review
- Analyzes last 4 quarters beat/miss history
- Presents consensus estimates (EPS, revenue)
- Identifies key metrics Wall Street watches
- Breaks down segment performance
- Summarizes management guidance
- Shows options market implied move
- Analyzes historical price reactions
- Models bull and bear case scenarios
- Recommends: buy before/sell before/wait

**When to use**:
- Before upcoming earnings report
- After earnings to analyze results
- Planning earnings season strategy
- Deciding whether to hold through earnings

**Output**: Pre-earnings research brief with decision summary

---

### 5. BlackRock Portfolio Construction (`/blackrock-portfolio-construction`)

**What it does**: Builds custom portfolios from scratch
- Designs exact asset allocation (stocks/bonds/alternatives)
- Recommends specific ETFs/funds with tickers
- Labels core vs. satellite positions
- Projects expected annual return range
- Estimates maximum drawdown potential
- Creates rebalancing schedule and rules
- Develops tax efficiency strategy
- Builds dollar cost averaging plan
- Selects performance benchmark
- Drafts investment policy statement

**When to use**:
- Starting new portfolio
- Major portfolio restructuring
- After significant life event
- Creating systematic investment plan

**Output**: Investment policy document with allocation blueprint

---

### 6. Citadel Technical Analysis (`/citadel-technical-analysis`)

**What it does**: Full technical analysis for trade timing
- Identifies trend on daily/weekly/monthly timeframes
- Determines key support and resistance levels
- Analyzes moving averages (50/100/200-day)
- Interprets RSI, MACD, Bollinger Bands
- Analyzes volume trends
- Identifies chart patterns (head & shoulders, etc.)
- Calculates Fibonacci retracement levels
- Recommends entry, stop-loss, profit targets
- Calculates risk-to-reward ratio
- Provides rating: strong buy to strong sell

**When to use**:
- Timing entry or exit
- Determining stop-loss levels
- Assessing current technical setup
- Confirming fundamental analysis

**Output**: Trade setup report card with clear plan

### 7. Harvard Dividend Strategy (`/harvard-dividend-strategy`)

- Breaks down sector diversification
- Estimates 5-year dividend growth rate
- Models DRIP reinvestment (10-year projection)
- Summarizes tax implications
- Ranks from safest to most aggressive

**When to use**:
- Building income portfolio
- Retirement income planning
- Seeking passive cash flow
- Comparing dividend stocks

**Output**: Income portfolio blueprint with projection tables

## Tips for Best Results

### Prepare Your Data

**What you'll need**:
- Ticker symbols for stocks you're analyzing
- Your current portfolio holdings (for risk analysis)
- Your investment goals and constraints
- Time horizon and risk tolerance

**Recommended approach**:
- Start with screening skills (Goldman)
- Narrow to 3-5 candidates for deep analysis
- Use valuation and competitive analysis
- Check macro environment fit
- Assess risk before committing capital

### Invoke Skills Effectively

**Good invocation**:
```
/goldman-stock-screener

Investment Profile:
- Risk Tolerance: Moderate
- Investment Amount: $50,000
- Time Horizon: 5-10 years
- Preferred Sectors: Technology, Healthcare
- Criteria: P/E < 25, Revenue growth > 15%, Market cap > $10B
```

**Also acceptable**:
```
/morgan-dcf-valuation

Ticker: AAPL
Company: Apple Inc.
Focus: Conservative assumptions for margin of safety
```

### Customize Analysis Depth

Most skills can be adapted:
- Specify focus areas (e.g., "focus on dividend safety" for dividend strategy)
- Request more/fewer picks (e.g., "top 20 stocks instead of 10")
- Adjust timeframes (e.g., "3-year outlook instead of 5")
- Add constraints (e.g., "ESG-focused only", "avoid certain sectors")

## Understanding Skill Outputs

### Output Structure

Each skill generates analysis in consistent format:
- **Executive Summary**: Key findings at top
- **Detailed Analysis**: Step-by-step breakdown
- **Tables and Metrics**: Structured data
- **Recommendations**: Actionable next steps
- **Disclaimers**: Risk warnings

### Interpreting Results

**High-confidence findings**:
- Based on current market data
- Supported by multiple metrics
- Consistent across analyses
- Explicitly stated in analysis

**Medium-confidence findings**:
- Based on projections or estimates
- Single-metric driven
- Requires monitoring

**Low-confidence/Speculative**:
- Long-term forecasts
- Scenario-based
- Dependent on many assumptions
- Clearly labeled as such

### Data Limitations

- Analysis may use data as of knowledge cutoff date
- Market data changes constantly - verify current prices
- Projections are estimates, not guarantees
- Historical patterns may not continue

## Best Practices

### For Stock Analysis

1. **Use multiple skills** - Don't rely on single analysis
2. **Start with screening** - Goldman screener to find candidates
3. **Validate competitively** - Bain analysis vs. peers
4. **Value properly** - Morgan Stanley DCF for intrinsic value
5. **Time entry** - Citadel technical analysis
6. **Size appropriately** - Bridgewater risk analysis

### For Portfolio Management

1. **Understand macro first** - McKinsey analysis for environment
2. **Build systematically** - BlackRock portfolio construction
3. **Stress test** - Bridgewater risk analysis regularly
4. **Rebalance systematically** - Follow policy, not emotions
5. **Monitor continuously** - Review quarterly minimum

### For Risk Management

1. **Never invest more than you can afford to lose**
2. **Diversify across stocks, sectors, and asset classes**
3. **Use stop-losses** - Define exit before entry
4. **Size positions based on conviction and risk**
5. **Hedge when appropriate** - Consider downside protection
6. **Keep cash buffer** - For opportunities and emergencies

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

**Issue**: Analysis seems generic
- **Solution**: Provide more specific criteria and context

**Issue**: Data appears outdated
- **Solution**: Note that analysis may use data as of knowledge cutoff; verify current data independently

**Issue**: Recommendations conflict between skills
- **Solution**: Different frameworks emphasize different factors; use judgment to synthesize

**Issue**: Can't find certain financial data
- **Solution**: Some data may not be in training; you may need to provide specific numbers

### Getting Better Results

**Improve analysis quality**:
1. Be specific with your requirements
2. Provide context about your goals
3. State your constraints clearly
4. Ask for focus on particular aspects
5. Request sensitivity analysis for key assumptions

**Improve output usefulness**:
1. Specify desired output format
2. Request specific tables or charts
3. Ask for comparison to benchmarks
4. Request scenario analysis
