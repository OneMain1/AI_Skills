## Synthesis (400 words maximum)

### Established Consensus (~100 words)

[Write flowing paragraphs that synthesize what the field collectively agrees on. Cite at least 2 papers supporting each major claim. Write declaratively without hedging language.]

There is widespread acceptance that the relationship between A and B operates through mechanism C (Wilson, 2021; Garcia, 2022).]

### Active Debates (~100 words)

[Write flowing paragraphs describing substantive disagreements WITHOUT naming individual papers. Focus on the intellectual debate itself and why it matters.]

[Example: The field is sharply divided on whether X causes Y or merely correlates with it, with some researchers emphasizing experimental evidence while others point to the limitations of controlled settings. Another unresolved debate concerns the generalizability of findings from domain A to domain B, where disagreement centers on whether mechanisms are universal or context-dependent. A third area of active discussion involves measurement approaches, particularly whether construct X is best captured through self-report or behavioral observation.]

### Strongest Evidence (~100 words)

[Write flowing paragraphs about what claims are most reliably supported by rigorous, replicated evidence. Focus on evidence quality and consistency across studies.]

[Example: The most robust finding is that X significantly affects Y, demonstrated through experimental manipulations (Author, Year), large-scale surveys (Author, Year), and computational modeling (Author, Year). Equally well-supported is the moderating role of Z, consistently observed across diverse populations and measurement approaches. Strong convergent evidence from multiple methodologies establishes that mechanism M underlies the relationship, with qualitative, quantitative, and mixed-methods studies reaching concordant conclusions.]

### The Key Open Question (~80 words)

[Build up to the single most important unanswered question. Explain why it matters, then state the question clearly and specifically.]

[Example: Despite substantial progress in understanding how X influences Y, the literature has not resolved whether these effects persist longitudinally or dissipate over time. This gap is critical because interventions based on short-term findings may fail in practice if effects are transient. Resolving this would fundamentally alter both theoretical models and practical applications. The key unanswered question is: Do the effects of X on Y remain stable beyond six months, and if not, what mechanisms explain their decay?]

[State as a clear, specific question that could guide a research program]

This synthesis is suitable for:
- ✓ Literature review introduction or conclusion sections

This synthesis is NOT suitable for:
- ✗ Replacing detailed reading of individual papers

## Next Steps

Based on this synthesis:

1. **For your literature review**:
   - Use "Established Consensus" for background/foundations section
   - Use "Active Debates" to set up your contribution
   - Use "Key Open Question" to motivate your research

2. **For your research design**:
   - Address the key open question directly, or
   - Resolve one of the active debates with better evidence, or
   - Extend consensus findings to new contexts

3. **For further analysis**:
   - Run `/assumption-killer` to examine if consensus rests on untested assumptions
   - Run `/gap-scanner` to identify multiple research opportunities beyond the single key question
   - Run `/methodology-audit` to ensure your design improves on existing methods
